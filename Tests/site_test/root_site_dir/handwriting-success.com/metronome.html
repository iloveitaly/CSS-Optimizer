What is it?

The Interactive Metrnome (IM) program is used to enchance a person's motor sequencing, timing, and coordination skills. These skills can also promote learning! They are involved in sensory motor regulation and the ability to focus and attend.

The Interactive Metronome is a PC-based interactive version of the traditional music metronome. Hand and foot sensors are used to objectively measure a users performance as he attempts to move to a fixed metronome beat. The user performs thirteen different movement patterns to address coordination, motor senquencing and timing skills.

The standard training program is organized into 15 sessions which are ideally completed in a 3-5 week period. The IM's patented auditory guidance system progressively challenges the students to stay closer to the beat. Trainees are rewarded for each "personal best" score achieved along their path to their ultimate performance goals. Trainees may require additional sessions to reach their goals. Some benefit from pre-training "synchronization" sessions to assist them in meeting the demands of the standard training program.
